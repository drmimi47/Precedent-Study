What is HAQUE TAN

TAN, Ling

Ling Tan is an architect-trained designer and creative technologist whose work explores how technology can foster dialogue and collective action within the built environment. Known for blending design, data science, and social engagement, she collaborates with communities around the world to address urban challenges like air quality, safety, and equity through participatory projects such as Low Carbon Chinatown and the SUPERPOWER! GROWING RIVERSIDERS

An URBAN GARDEN Co-Created by 100 Families

During the isolation of London's 2021 lockdown, Growing Riversiders explored how remote communities could still come together to shape public space. Blending digital coordination with hands-on care, the project invited 100 families to each nurture four plants at home, plants that would later become individual "pixels" in a large-scale living image reflecting the community's shared identity. Rooted in the everyday act of caregiving, the project culminated in a collective installation of over 400 plants at the Wilds Ecology Centre, where families assembled the landscape in staggered time slots, transforming personal efforts into a public, collaborative intervention.


Digital platform and Mobile Web App

To support the community's involvement, a custom digital platform was developed to help participants grow, track, and share their plants' progress from home. The platform also allowed families to experiment with possible layouts for the final installation, which would eventually take the form of a honey bee‚Äîa powerful symbol of collaboration and sustainability. This living artwork became a celebration of local environmental values and collective care. Remarkably, every single plant was returned to the site for the final assembly, reflecting a strong sense of shared responsibility and a successful vision of co-creation at Barking Riverside.
